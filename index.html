My Story

My Story

When I was around 12 years old, my dad noticed how curious I was about electronics, so he surprised me with a personal computer. Back then, it was the very early days of home computing, and I was instantly hooked. I didn’t just want to use the computer — I wanted to understand it, to build games of my own. That curiosity pulled me into the world of programming and software engineering. I spent endless hours experimenting, learning efficient design, logic, critical thinking, how to debug and solve problems, spot patterns, and think in a systematic way. Those skills among many other would later prove to be super useful in my professional career.

At the same time, I was also inspired by my older brother, a highly successful CFO, whose world of finance and strategy fascinated me. That influence led me to business school, where I dove into the business world with energy and ambition. But soon after graduating, I found myself buried in data and repetitive manual tasks. That’s when it clicked — I could go back to my programming roots and use those skills to automate the boring stuff and make work more efficient.

My colleagues quickly recognized my capabilities, and over the years, more and more of them began reaching out to me for help with their digital challenges. This gave me a unique, cross-functional view of the strategic inner workings of the corporation and the common issues, challenges, and painpoints shared by most business people. For me that was a fantatstic opportunity to learn. I wasn’t just solving problems for myself—I was supporting teams across multiple departments. That experience taught me how to collaborate effectively, navigate corporate dynamics, and work with diverse stakeholders. It also motivated me to live up to my growing reputation, which became a strong incentive to dive deep into technical R&D. As a result, I gained extensive strategic, business, technical, and interpersonal hands-on experience through self-driven exploration, trial and error, and practical problem-solving.

One day, after I built a particularly effective automation tool at work that saved enormous time, my boss pulled me aside and said, “You’re clearly very talented at this — and even more importantly, you seem to be very passionate about it. Why not make this your new career?” That simple comment stuck with me. It gave me the encouragement I needed to take what I had always considered a side passion and turn it into something more.

I decided to take a leap of faith and leave behind a very sucessful career in Financial Planning and Analysis (FP&A) to pursue a career change to Data Science, Software Engineering, and Analytics — and that’s when I enrolled at the University of Toronto to formally pursue my dream. While studying there, I was fortunate to be noticed by one of my professors, who also worked in the corporate world. He saw my potential and invited me to join his team to work on real-world data science projects in the telecommunications industry. That opportunity gave me the hands-on experience I needed and ultimately kickstarted my new career in technology! :-)
